1. What DIRECT_BOT does

DIRECT_BOT receives Instagram Direct messages sent to @twocomms and helps customers with clothing-related questions. It can answer about product availability, sizes, colors, delivery, payment, order status, returns, and handoff to a human manager.

The bot does not post on behalf of users, does not access unrelated Instagram content, does not sell data, and does not message users outside the customer support conversation allowed by Meta's messaging rules.

3. Why the admin dashboard is not public

The operational bot dashboard contains private controls and customer support data: start/stop controls, API credential source settings, AI configuration, prompt/knowledge settings, sender allowlist, customer list, conversation snippets, and diagnostic logs.

For security and privacy, the dashboard is restricted to authenticated TwoComms administrators. Public access to these controls would allow unauthorized users to stop the bot, change settings, view customer data, or disrupt support operations.

Reviewers can use this public page, the privacy policy, terms, data deletion page, screenshots/screencast, and any reviewer credentials provided in Meta App Review. The actual control panel remains private by design.

4. User-facing behavior

  • A customer sends a Direct message to the official Instagram account @twocomms.
  • DIRECT_BOT drafts or sends a customer support reply using the configured twocomms support rules and Google Gemini Flash AI.
  • If the conversation requires a human decision, the conversation can be handed to a manager.
  • Order-related data is used only to prepare or support the customer's order.

5. Reviewer testing flow

To verify the requested Instagram messaging permission, reviewers can follow the same flow shown in the App Review screencast:

  • Open the official Instagram account @twocomms.
  • Send a Direct message such as: "Hello, what sizes are available for black T-shirts and how can I order?"
  • Observe that DIRECT_BOT receives the Direct message, uses the customer support context, and replies with product/order guidance.
  • In the private owner dashboard, an authorized TwoComms administrator can see the conversation, bot status, handoff controls, and logs.
  • If Meta needs to access the private dashboard, TwoComms should provide a temporary reviewer account only inside the Meta App Review submission notes. The account should be removed after review.
